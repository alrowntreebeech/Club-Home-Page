Human Chess Society

ABOUT

A club for those who want to enjoy the cerebral aspect of chess and the sense of comaraderie, but are suffering from bloodlust. This is the almagamtion of when brawn meets brains and is the closest experience one will get to real warfare, without being on a battlefield. Test your mettle with other likeminded people who enjoy the companionship and gore that comes with Human Chess.

I've never made so many friends as I have in the Human Chess Society. Nothing brings you closer together than a life-threatening situation, and any friend I've lost on the board has been replaced with another. ~ Warren Peace (New-Member)

The history of human chess is one shrouded in mystery. Believed to have its roots in the paleolithic area where hunters would strategise ways of trapping their prey, it soon evolved into the game we recongise nowadays as humans developed culturally and socially. Indeed, human chess is believed to be the pre-cursor to war, where warring settlements and early nations would decide the victor in a game of human chess. Although this did not last as more intelligent leaders with bigger armies realised that fighting weaker opponents on a level playing field was actually detrimental to them, human chess is still used as a military training technique for generals and leaders. Great human chess players include Alexander the Great, Julius Ceaser and Napoleon Bonaparte.

The history of human chess has been carefully collected by our amateur historians, and although our findings have faced univerisal criticism and derision from historians and politicians, we leave it up to our current and future members to decide whether to listen to these so called "historical experts". Today, human chess is seen as an underground cult, but it's influence has spread, even featuring in popular programmes such as The Simpsons, which has perfectly captured the brutality and fun of human chess.
